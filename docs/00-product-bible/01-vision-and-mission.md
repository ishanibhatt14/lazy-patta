# Vision & Mission

## Vision

A world where distance never ends a family card game.

Indian families are spread across cities and continents, but the games that
defined our childhoods — played on the floor during Diwali, on trains, on lazy
Sunday afternoons — mostly stayed at home. Lazy Patta brings that table online so
a grandparent in Ahmedabad and a grandchild in Toronto can play **Gadha Chor**
together in five taps, and it feels like home.

## Mission

Make the traditional card games Indian families grew up playing **easy to play
together from anywhere** — warm, premium, and unmistakably _not_ a casino.

## The one-sentence promise

> The Indian card games families grew up playing, made easy to play together from anywhere.

## The emotional target

Every design and engineering decision is judged against a single feeling:

| We want players to think… | We never want players to think… |
|---|---|
| "This feels like playing cards at my grandparents' house." | "This is just another Teen Patti / rummy app." |
| "I could hand this to my mom and she'd get it." | "Where's the catch / what are they selling me?" |
| "That donkey reveal made me laugh." | "This feels like a slot machine." |

That emotional difference — nostalgia and family warmth — is far harder for a
competitor to copy than a set of game rules. It is our moat.

## What we are

- A **family game night**, hosted online.
- **Gujarati-first in personality**, broadly Indian and diaspora-friendly in reach.
- A **platform** designed from day one to hold many games (Gadha Chor first, then
  Lal Satti, Judgement, Mendicot, 3-2-5, Bluff…), not a single-game app.
- **Premium and warm**: Airbnb-grade polish, Nintendo/Supercell delight, expressed
  through Indian folk craft rather than neon.

## What we are not — non-negotiables

These are hard lines, not preferences. They protect both the brand and store
compliance (see [decisions-log](./decisions-log.md) D-02, D-24):

1. **Not gambling.** No cash, betting, wagering, prize pools, or redeemable
   currency. Not Teen Patti. Not Poker.
2. **No fake economy.** No gold coins, chips, loot boxes, or "win big" language.
   We celebrate pairs, rematches, and family — never a balance going up.
3. **No casino aesthetic.** No felt-and-neon, no slot imagery, no glossy gold.
4. **No open communication in MVP.** Preset reactions only; no free text or voice.
   Safety-by-default for an audience that includes kids and grandparents.
5. **No dark patterns.** No forced accounts to try the game, no manipulative
   streaks, no pay-to-win. Guests can play the computer immediately.

## How we know it's working (north-star signals)

The full KPI tree lives in [07 · Analytics & KPIs](../07-product-strategy/analytics-and-kpis.md).
At the vision level, we're succeeding when:

- A first-time guest completes a full Gadha Chor match against bots **and comes back**.
- Families play **multi-device, multi-generation** live rooms (the diaspora use case).
- Players describe it, unprompted, in **emotional / nostalgic** terms — not feature terms.

## Design principles (the tie-breakers)

When two good options compete, prefer the one that is:

1. **Simple** — start playing fast; make every turn obvious.
2. **Warm** — folk craft, family language, gentle humor over spectacle.
3. **Accessible** — large cards and controls; works for grandparents and kids.
4. **Honest** — private things stay private; the server never lies; no dark patterns.
5. **Scalable** — build the reusable system, not the one-off screen.
6. **Delightful** — a little joy in every interaction, never at the cost of clarity.
